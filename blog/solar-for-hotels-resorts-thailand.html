If you run a hotel, resort, or guesthouse in Thailand, you already know your biggest operational headache: electricity bills. Air conditioning alone can account for 40–60% of total energy consumption, and with PEA rates climbing year after year, many hospitality businesses are seeing their margins squeezed.

The good news? Solar energy is now the single most effective way to slash those costs — and with the PPA model, you don't even need to invest a single baht upfront.

1. Hotel Energy Costs in Thailand: The Problem

Hospitality is one of the most energy-intensive industries. In Thailand's tropical climate, hotels face a perfect storm of high consumption:

Property Type Monthly Bill (Est.) Primary Consumers
Small guesthouse (10 rooms) ฿30,000–฿50,000 A/C, water heating, lighting
Mid-range resort (30 rooms) ฿80,000–฿120,000 A/C, pool pumps, laundry, kitchen
Large hotel (80+ rooms) ฿150,000–฿300,000 Central A/C, F&B, common areas
Luxury resort with pool villas ฿200,000–฿500,000+ Private pools, spa, chillers

With PEA commercial rates at ฿5.50–฿6.50/kWh (including demand charges and Ft surcharge), electricity often represents the second or third largest operating expense after staff salaries and rent.

The Rising Cost Trend

Thailand's Ft (fuel adjustment) charge has been volatile, spiking from ฿-0.15 in 2021 to over ฿0.93 in 2023 before settling around ฿0.39 in 2026. Every satang increase translates to thousands of baht per month for a hotel. Solar locks in your energy cost at a fixed rate for 25+ years.

2. Why Solar Makes Sense for Hospitality

Hotels are actually ideal candidates for solar energy — more so than most other business types. Here's why:

Peak Demand Matches Solar Production

Hotels consume the most electricity during the day — exactly when solar panels produce the most energy. Air conditioning peaks from 10 AM to 4 PM. Pool pumps run during daylight hours. Kitchens operate for lunch service. This near-perfect alignment means 80–95% self-consumption rates, maximizing the value of every unit generated.

Large Roof Area

Hotels have enormous roof space — often 500–2,000+ m² — that sits completely unused. A standard hotel roof can accommodate a 50–200 kW system easily, generating enough electricity to offset the majority of daytime consumption.

Consistent Year-Round Demand

Unlike seasonal businesses, hotels in popular Thai destinations maintain relatively steady occupancy (60–90%) year-round. This means consistent, predictable savings from solar — not just during peak season.

Flat Roofs = Easy Installation

Most Thai commercial buildings, including hotels, have flat concrete roofs. This makes solar installation faster, cheaper, and allows optimal panel tilting (10–15° facing south) for maximum generation.

3. The PPA Model: Go Solar with Zero Investment

The Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) is a game-changer for hotels that want solar but don't want (or can't afford) the upfront capital investment.

How It Works

  1. TM Energy installs the solar system on your roof at no cost to you
  2. We own and maintain the system for the contract duration (typically 15–20 years)
  3. You buy the solar electricity at a fixed rate of ฿4.50/kWh — guaranteed for the contract term
  4. You save immediately — the difference between ฿4.50 and your PEA rate (฿5.50–฿6.50) goes straight to your bottom line
  5. At contract end, you can purchase the system at residual value or renew

PPA vs. Direct Purchase Comparison

Frequently Asked Questions

Can solar panels handle the energy needs of a large hotel?

Solar typically covers 40–70% of a hotel's daytime electricity needs, depending on roof space and consumption patterns. The remaining energy comes from the grid. Even partial coverage translates to significant savings — ฿50,000–฿150,000/month for mid-to-large properties.

Will solar panels damage my hotel's roof?

No. Modern mounting systems are designed to protect your roof. For flat concrete roofs (common in Thai hotels), we use ballasted systems that don't require drilling. For other roof types, we use waterproof mounting hardware with 25-year warranties.

How long does installation take? Will it disrupt guests?

A typical 50–100 kW hotel installation takes 5–10 working days. Work happens on the roof with minimal noise and no disruption to guest areas. We can schedule installation during low-occupancy periods if preferred.

What about the rainy season?

Solar panels generate electricity year-round, even on cloudy days. During the rainy season (Oct–Dec in the Gulf, May–Oct on the Andaman coast), production drops to about 50–70% of peak — but hotels also tend to have lower occupancy during these months, balancing out nicely.

Do I need a building permit for a hotel solar installation?

Rooftop solar systems under 200 kW don't require a building permit in Thailand. You do need a PEA grid connection agreement for net billing, which TM Energy handles as part of our service.

What's the minimum roof space needed?

You need approximately 5–6 m² per kW of solar capacity. A 50 kW system requires about 250–300 m² of unshaded roof space. Most hotels have more than enough.
