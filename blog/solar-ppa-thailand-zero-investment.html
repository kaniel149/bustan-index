The PPA model has transformed solar adoption worldwide, and it's now available in Thailand. For businesses tired of watching their PEA bills climb year after year, it's the lowest-risk way to go solar.

1. What Is a Solar PPA?

A Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) is a contract where a solar company (like Bustan Energy) installs, owns, and maintains a solar system on your roof — and you simply buy the electricity it produces at a rate lower than what you pay PEA.

The Simple Version

  • You provide: Your roof space
  • We provide: The solar system, installation, maintenance, monitoring — everything
  • You pay: A fixed rate per kWh for the solar electricity (lower than PEA)
  • You save: The difference between our rate and PEA's rate — immediately, month one

Think of it like this: instead of paying PEA ฿6 for every unit of electricity, you pay Bustan Energy ฿4.50 for the solar portion. The ฿1.50 difference per unit goes straight to your bottom line.

2. How It Works: Step by Step

  1. Energy audit: We analyze your last 12 months of PEA bills to understand your consumption patterns
  2. Site assessment: Our engineers evaluate your roof — size, condition, orientation, shading
  3. System design: We design the optimal system to maximize your savings based on your actual usage
  4. Contract signing: We agree on the PPA rate, term, and conditions
  5. Installation: We install the system at our cost — typically 1–2 weeks for commercial projects
  6. PEA connection: We handle all grid connection paperwork and meter installation
  7. Start saving: Your system goes live. You receive two bills — a reduced PEA bill plus a Bustan Energy bill for solar electricity. Combined, they're less than your old PEA bill alone

4. Who Qualifies for a Solar PPA?

Ideal PPA Candidates

  • Monthly electricity bill: ฿30,000+ (higher = better ROI for both parties)
  • Roof space: Minimum 150 m² of unshaded, structurally sound roof
  • Roof ownership: You own the building or have a long-term lease (10+ years remaining)
  • Daytime consumption: High daytime electricity use (ideal for offices, factories, hotels, malls)
  • Creditworthiness: Established business with stable operations

Who PPA Works Best For

  • Hotels & resorts — High daytime A/C and pool pump loads (see our hotel solar guide)
  • Factories & warehouses — Large roof areas, consistent consumption (see our commercial solar guide)
  • Shopping malls & retail — Peak usage aligns perfectly with solar production
  • Schools & universities — Budget-conscious with large roof areas
  • Hospitals & clinics — 24/7 operation benefits from reduced rates

When PPA May Not Be the Best Option

  • Small residential properties — Bills under ฿5,000/month make PPA economics challenging
  • Short-term leases — Need 10+ years remaining on your lease
  • Buildings with heavy shading — Trees, adjacent buildings blocking sunlight
  • Poor roof condition — Roof may need repair/replacement before solar installation

For smaller properties, direct purchase often makes more financial sense. See our savings calculator for comparison.

5. Contract Terms & What to Watch For

Typical PPA Terms in Thailand

Term Typical Range Bustan Energy Standard
Contract duration 10–25 years 15–20 years
PPA rate ฿3.80–฿5.00/kWh ฿4.50/kWh (fixed)
Annual escalation 0–3% 0% (truly fixed)
Performance guarantee 80–90% of design output 85% guaranteed
Maintenance Included or separate Fully included
Insurance Varies Included (equipment + liability)
End-of-term options Purchase, renew, or remove All three options available

Key Things to Watch For

  • Escalation clauses: Some providers include 1–3% annual rate increases. Bustan Energy offers a truly fixed rate — no surprises
  • Performance guarantees: Ensure the provider guarantees a minimum energy output. If the system underperforms, they should compensate you
  • Early termination: Understand the buyout formula if you want to end the contract early or purchase the system
  • Roof maintenance access: The contract should specify how roof repairs are handled (who moves panels, who pays)
  • Insurance & liability: The provider should carry insurance covering equipment damage and third-party liability

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens if I sell the building during the PPA contract?

The PPA contract is typically transferable to the new owner. This can actually be a selling point — the new owner inherits guaranteed electricity savings from day one. We help facilitate the transfer process.

Can I cancel the PPA early?

Yes, but there's usually an early termination fee based on the remaining contract value. Alternatively, you can buy out the system at a calculated residual value. The exact terms are specified upfront in the contract.

What if the solar system doesn't produce enough energy?

Bustan Energy guarantees a minimum annual production (85% of design output). If we fall short, we compensate you for the difference. You only pay for the electricity you actually receive.

Who handles maintenance and repairs?

Bustan Energy handles all maintenance, monitoring, and repairs at no additional cost. This includes panel cleaning, inverter servicing, and any component replacements. We monitor system performance 24/7 remotely.

Is a PPA the same as a solar lease?

Similar but different. In a PPA, you pay per kWh of electricity produced. In a lease, you pay a fixed monthly fee regardless of production. PPAs are more common in Thailand and generally more favorable for the customer since you only pay for actual output.

Does the PPA rate ever increase?

Bustan Energy offers a truly fixed rate for the full contract term — no annual escalation. Some other providers include 1–3% annual increases, so always check the fine print. Our ฿4.50/kWh rate is locked in for the entire contract.

Can I combine PPA with net billing?

In a PPA arrangement, the system owner (Bustan Energy) typically handles net billing. Any excess electricity exported to the grid benefits the system economics and helps keep your PPA rate competitive. The specifics depend on the contract structure.
