Everyone asks the same question before going solar: "How much will I actually save?" Not hypothetical savings — real numbers based on real Thai electricity rates.

This guide breaks down exactly how solar savings work in Thailand, using current PEA rates and real installation data from our projects across Koh Phangan and Southern Thailand.

1. Understanding PEA Electricity Rates in 2026

Before calculating solar savings, you need to understand what you're currently paying. Thailand's electricity pricing has several components:

*Ft (Fuel Adjustment) charge varies quarterly. ฿0.39/kWh is the current rate as of early 2026. Add 7% VAT for final price.

What You Actually Pay Per kWh

When you add base rate + Ft charge + service charge + VAT, most Thai households pay an effective rate of ฿5.00–฿6.00 per kWh. Heavy users (villas, businesses) pay even more due to higher tiers.

For a complete breakdown of your electricity bill, see: Understanding Your Thai Electricity Bill.

💡 Why This Matters for Solar

Every kWh your solar panels produce that you consume directly replaces a kWh at ฿5–6. That's the real value of solar — not the export rate of ฿2.70. Self-consumption is where the money is.

2. Self-Consumption vs Export: The Critical Difference

This is the single most important concept in solar economics in Thailand:

What Happens Value per kWh Example
Self-Consumption
You use the solar electricity directly		 ฿5.00–฿6.00
(avoided PEA cost)		 Running AC during the day with solar
Export to Grid
Excess goes to PEA via net billing		 ~฿2.70
(PEA buy-back rate)		 Panels producing while you're away

The difference is nearly double. A system with 80% self-consumption earns almost twice the value per kWh compared to a system that exports 80%.

How to Maximize Self-Consumption

  • Right-size your system: Don't over-install. Match system size to your daytime consumption.
  • Shift loads to daytime: Run pool pumps, water heaters, washing machines 10am–3pm.
  • Smart timers: Program appliances to run during peak solar hours.
  • Battery storage: Capture excess for evening use. See our battery guide.
  • EV charging: Charge your electric vehicle during the day.

5. Hidden Savings Most People Miss

Property Value Increase

Properties with solar installations sell for 3–5% more in Thailand's real estate market. On a ฿10M villa, that's ฿300,000–฿500,000 in added value — nearly paying for the system itself.

AC Efficiency Bonus

Solar panels shade your roof, reducing attic temperatures by 5–7°C. This means your air conditioning works less hard, saving an additional 5–10% on cooling costs even beyond the direct solar savings.

Protection Against Rate Increases

Thailand's electricity rates have increased 35%+ since 2021. By going solar, you effectively lock in a portion of your electricity cost at ฿0 for 25+ years. The more rates increase, the more you save.

Green Marketing Value

For hotels and resorts, solar provides a tangible eco-friendly credential that attracts environmentally conscious travelers. Properties can market this as a competitive advantage — particularly strong in Koh Phangan's wellness and eco-tourism market.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the minimum electricity bill where solar makes sense?

Generally, if your monthly PEA bill is above ฿2,000, solar will provide positive ROI. The sweet spot is ฿5,000+/month where payback periods drop below 5 years.

Do savings decrease as panels age?

Solar panels degrade about 0.4–0.5% per year. After 25 years, they still produce ~87% of original capacity. This degradation is factored into our ROI calculations.

Are these savings guaranteed?

Solar production depends on weather and system maintenance. Our estimates are based on conservative averages from NASA solar irradiance data for Thailand. Actual savings may be higher in sunny years.

What about maintenance costs?

Solar systems require minimal maintenance: 1–2 cleanings per year (฿500–฿1,500 each) and an inverter replacement around year 12–15 (฿30,000–฿60,000). Total lifetime maintenance is typically under 5% of total savings.

Can I finance a solar system?

Yes! TM Energy offers PPA (Power Purchase Agreement) options where you pay zero upfront and simply buy solar electricity at a discounted rate. See our PPA guide.
