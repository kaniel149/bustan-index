Understanding Your Thai Electricity Bill: A Complete Breakdown

📅 March 16, 2026 ✍️ TM Energy Team ⏱️ 9 min read

🔑 Key Takeaways

  • Your Thai electricity bill has 4 main components: base rate, Ft charge, service charge, and VAT
  • The Ft (fuel adjustment) charge fluctuates every 4 months — it's the most volatile part of your bill
  • Residential rates are progressive (tiered) — the more you use, the more you pay per unit
  • The effective rate for most households is ฿4.50–฿5.50/kWh; businesses pay ฿5.50–฿6.50/kWh
  • Solar energy reduces your bill by attacking the highest-tier consumption first

If you've ever looked at your Thai electricity bill and felt confused, you're not alone. Between the tiered base rates, the mysterious "Ft charge," service fees, and VAT, it can feel like you need a finance degree to understand what you're paying for.

This guide breaks down every component of your PEA (Provincial Electricity Authority) bill so you know exactly where your money goes — and where solar can save you the most.

1. Your Bill at a Glance

Every Thai electricity bill consists of these components:

Component What It Is % of Total Bill
Base Rate (ค่าพลังงานไฟฟ้า) The per-unit charge for electricity consumed 65–75%
Ft Charge (ค่า Ft) Fuel adjustment surcharge — fluctuates quarterly 10–20%
Service Charge (ค่าบริการ) Fixed monthly fee for grid connection 1–3%
VAT (ภาษีมูลค่าเพิ่ม) 7% value-added tax on total 7%

For commercial customers, there's an additional demand charge based on your peak power draw — we'll cover that in section 5.

2. Base Rate: Tiered Pricing Explained

Thailand uses a progressive (tiered) pricing system for residential customers. The more electricity you use, the higher the rate per unit. This is crucial to understand because solar saves you money at the highest tier first.

Residential Rates (PEA, 2026)

Tier Units (kWh/month) Rate (฿/kWh) Notes
1 0–15 Free Lifeline rate (if using ≤50 units total)
2 16–25 ฿2.3488
3 26–35 ฿2.9882
4 36–100 ฿3.2405
5 101–150 ฿3.6237
6 151–400 ฿3.7171 Most households fall here
7 401+ ฿4.2218 Highest rate — solar saves most here

Why Tiers Matter for Solar

When you install solar, the electricity you generate offsets your consumption from the top tier down. If you normally use 800 units/month and solar generates 400 units, you eliminate the most expensive 400 units (Tier 7 at ฿4.22/kWh) — not the cheapest ones.

This means your effective savings per kWh from solar are higher than the average rate on your bill. It's one of the hidden benefits of solar in a tiered pricing system.

Commercial Rates

Business customers have different rate structures depending on their connection type:

  • Small general service (< 30 kW): Progressive tiers similar to residential but higher
  • Medium general service (30–999 kW): Flat energy rate + demand charge
  • Large general service (≥ 1,000 kW): Time-of-use (TOU) rates — peak vs off-peak

3. The Ft Charge: Thailand's Hidden Variable

The Ft charge (ค่า Ft) — short for "fuel adjustment tariff" — is the most misunderstood and volatile part of your electricity bill. It's adjusted by the Energy Regulatory Commission (ERC) every 4 months based on:

  • Natural gas prices — the primary fuel for Thailand's power plants
  • Exchange rates — gas is priced in USD, so baht weakness increases costs
  • Power plant efficiency — older plants cost more to run
  • Government policy — subsidies can temporarily reduce the Ft charge

Ft Charge History

Period Ft Rate (฿/kWh) Direction
Jan 2021 -0.1532 Negative (subsidy)
Sep 2022 +0.9343 Massive spike (energy crisis)
Jan 2024 +0.3974 Still elevated
Jan 2026 +0.3900 Stabilizing but elevated

The Ft charge is applied to every unit you consume. For a household using 500 units/month, the Ft charge alone adds approximately ฿195/month at current rates. For a business using 10,000 units, that's ฿3,900/month — just from the Ft surcharge.

Why This Matters for Solar

Solar electricity has no Ft charge. When you generate your own power, you avoid both the base rate AND the Ft charge. As Ft charges remain elevated or increase, your solar savings grow proportionally.

4. Service Charge & VAT

Service Charge (ค่าบริการ)

A fixed monthly fee charged by PEA for maintaining your grid connection:

  • Residential: ฿38.22/month
  • Small commercial: ฿46.16/month
  • Medium/large commercial: ฿228.17–฿312.24/month

The service charge applies regardless of consumption — even if you use zero electricity, you pay this fee. Solar doesn't eliminate it, but it's a tiny fraction of most bills.

VAT (ภาษีมูลค่าเพิ่ม)

Thailand applies 7% VAT on the total of base rate + Ft charge + service charge. This is standard and non-negotiable. However, since solar reduces your base rate and Ft charges, your VAT amount also decreases proportionally.

5. Demand Charges (Commercial Customers)

If your business has a connection of 30 kW or more, you face an additional demand charge — and this is where many businesses get surprised.

What Is Demand Charge?

The demand charge is based on your peak power draw (in kW) during the billing period, not your total consumption (in kWh). PEA measures your highest 15-minute average demand in each billing cycle.

Connection Type Demand Rate Example (100 kW peak)
Medium (30–999 kW) ฿132.93/kW/month ฿13,293/month
Large (≥ 1,000 kW) ฿221.50/kW/month (on-peak) ฿22,150/month

How Solar Affects Demand Charges

Solar can reduce your demand charge by lowering your peak draw from the grid. If your peak demand occurs during sunny hours (which is common for most businesses), solar panels supply part of that demand, reducing your measured peak. Combined with battery storage, you can "peak shave" even more effectively.

6. How to Read Your PEA Bill

Here's a section-by-section guide to your PEA bill:

  1. Account number (หมายเลขผู้ใช้ไฟ): Your unique PEA customer ID — you'll need this for net billing applications
  2. Meter reading (เลขมิเตอร์): Previous and current readings — the difference is your consumption
  3. Units consumed (หน่วยที่ใช้): Total kWh for the billing period
  4. Base rate (ค่าพลังงานไฟฟ้า): Calculated using the tiered rates above
  5. Ft charge (ค่า Ft): Units × current Ft rate
  6. Service charge (ค่าบริการ): Fixed monthly fee
  7. Subtotal before VAT: Base + Ft + service
  8. VAT 7%: Applied to subtotal
  9. Total (ยอดรวม): Amount due
  10. Due date (กำหนดชำระ): Usually 10–15 days from bill date

Quick Calculation Example

For a household using 500 units/month:

  • First 150 units: calculated at lower tiers ≈ ฿476
  • Units 151–400: 250 × ฿3.7171 = ฿929
  • Units 401–500: 100 × ฿4.2218 = ฿422
  • Base rate total: ฿1,827
  • Ft charge: 500 × ฿0.39 = ฿195
  • Service charge: ฿38
  • Subtotal: ฿2,060
  • VAT (7%): ฿144
  • Total: ≈ ฿2,204 (effective rate: ฿4.41/kWh)

Frequently Asked Questions

Why is my electricity bill higher than my neighbor's for the same size house?

Several factors affect your bill: number and efficiency of air conditioners, water heater type (electric vs gas), pool pump hours, number of refrigerators, and usage habits. Air conditioning alone can account for 40–60% of a Thai household's bill.

What is the Ft charge and why does it change?

The Ft (fuel adjustment tariff) reflects the actual cost of fuel used to generate electricity, primarily natural gas. It's adjusted every 4 months by the ERC. When global gas prices rise or the baht weakens, the Ft charge increases. It was as low as -0.15 in 2021 and spiked to +0.93 in 2022.

What's the difference between PEA and MEA bills?

PEA (Provincial Electricity Authority) serves areas outside Bangkok, including Koh Phangan and all provinces. MEA (Metropolitan Electricity Authority) serves Bangkok, Nonthaburi, and Samut Prakan. The rate structures are similar but not identical. This guide focuses on PEA rates.

Can I switch to Time-of-Use (TOU) rates?

TOU rates are available for customers with smart meters. You pay less during off-peak hours (22:00–09:00) and more during peak hours (09:00–22:00). TOU can save money if you shift consumption to nighttime, but it's less beneficial for solar owners since solar generates during peak hours — when standard tiered rates already save you the most.

Why does my bill have a "minimum charge"?

PEA has a minimum monthly charge (around ฿38–฿312 depending on connection type). Even if you use zero electricity (e.g., vacant property), you pay this amount for maintaining your grid connection. Solar can reduce your bill to near this minimum, but not below it.

How can I reduce my electricity bill without solar?

Quick wins: use inverter air conditioners (30–50% more efficient), set A/C to 25–26°C, use LED lighting, clean A/C filters monthly, use timers on water heaters. For bigger savings, solar remains the most impactful single investment. See our savings guide.
