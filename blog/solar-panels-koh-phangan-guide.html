1. Why Koh Phangan Is Perfect for Solar Energy

Koh Phangan sits at approximately 9.7°N latitude in the Gulf of Thailand, placing it in one of Southeast Asia's most productive solar zones. Here's what makes the island ideal:

Island-Specific Advantages

  • High electricity costs: PEA rates on islands are the same as mainland, but many properties use more electricity due to air conditioning in tropical heat — making solar savings larger
  • Frequent power outages: Island grid can be unreliable. Solar + battery provides energy independence
  • Eco-conscious community: Koh Phangan's wellness and eco-tourism scene values sustainability — solar adds real property value
  • Growing EV adoption: Solar pairs perfectly with EV charging infrastructure

Self-Consumption Is King

  • Running pool pumps, air conditioning, and water heaters during peak solar hours (10am–3pm)
  • Charging EVs during the day
  • Scheduling washing machines and dryers for midday
  • Adding a battery storage system to capture excess for nighttime use

4. Net Billing: Sell Your Excess Power to PEA

Thailand's net billing program (sometimes confused with "net metering") allows you to sell excess solar electricity back to the Provincial Electricity Authority (PEA) at a rate of approximately ฿2.70 per kWh.

For a complete breakdown of how this works, read our detailed guide: Net Billing in Thailand: How It Works in 2026.

Quick Summary

  • You consume solar electricity first (free)
  • Excess goes to the grid — PEA credits your bill at ~฿2.70/kWh
  • Credits offset your nighttime electricity consumption
  • It's not 1:1 net metering (you buy at ฿5+ but sell at ฿2.70)
  • Application through PEA takes 2–4 weeks

5. Permits & Regulations on Koh Phangan

Good news: Thailand has streamlined solar regulations significantly.

What You Need

  • Rooftop systems under 200 kW: No building permit required. You only need PEA approval for grid connection.
  • PEA application: Required for net billing. Your installer handles this paperwork.
  • Structural assessment: Recommended (not legally required) to ensure your roof can handle the additional weight (~15 kg/m²).
  • Electrical inspection: PEA inspects the system before activating grid connection.

For Foreign Property Owners

If you own property through a Thai company (common for foreigners on Koh Phangan), the company can apply for net billing. You'll need:

  • Company registration documents
  • PEA account in the company name
  • Authorized signatory for the application

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do solar panels last on Koh Phangan?

Quality solar panels (Tier-1 brands) last 25–30 years with minimal degradation. They come with a 25-year performance warranty guaranteeing at least 80% output after 25 years. Inverters typically last 10–15 years.

Do solar panels work during the rainy season?

Yes! Solar panels work with any daylight, not just direct sunshine. During Koh Phangan's rainy season (Oct–Dec), production drops to about 50–70% of peak, but you still generate significant electricity. The other 9 months more than compensate.

Can I install solar panels on a rented property?

Technically yes, but you'd need the landlord's permission and a long enough lease to justify the investment. Alternatively, ask your landlord about a PPA arrangement where Bustan Energy owns the system and you buy the electricity at a discount. See our Solar PPA guide.

What happens during a power outage?

Standard grid-tied solar systems shut down during outages (for safety). If you want backup power, you need a battery storage system with an Emergency Power Supply (EPS) function. See our battery storage guide.

Is solar worth it for a small house?

If your monthly electricity bill is over ฿2,000, solar is likely worth it. A small 3 kW system costs ฿90,000–฿120,000 and can save you ฿1,500–฿2,500 per month, paying for itself in 4–5 years.

Do I need to clean the panels?

Koh Phangan's regular rainfall does a good job of keeping panels clean. We recommend a professional cleaning 1–2 times per year, especially after the dry season when dust accumulates. Cost: ฿500–฿1,500 per cleaning.
