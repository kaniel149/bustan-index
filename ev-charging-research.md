## 1. Thailand EV Market Overview 2025-2026

### Sales & Growth
- **2024 EV registrations:** ~150,000+ (BEV), up 112% YoY
- **2025 target:** 200,000+ BEV registrations
- **Cumulative EVs on Thai roads:** ~350,000 (as of early 2026)
- **Market share:** EVs = ~15% of new car sales (up from 3% in 2022)
- **Top sellers:** BYD Atto 3, BYD Dolphin, MG4, Tesla Model 3/Y, ORA Good Cat, Neta V

### Government Policy — 30@30
- Target: 30% of vehicles produced in Thailand to be EVs by 2030
- **EV3.0 incentives (extended to 2025):** ฿70,000-150,000 subsidy per BEV
- **EV3.5 (2024-2027):** Reduced subsidies but continued excise tax cuts
- Excise tax: 2% for BEV (vs 25-35% for ICE)
- Import duty: 0-40% depending on FTA status (China-Thailand FTA = 0% on many models)

### BOI Incentives for EV Infrastructure
- **Category 5.3.5:** EV charging stations — 5-year corporate income tax exemption
- **Category 5.3.7:** Battery/EV manufacturing — 8-year exemption
- Import duty exemption on machinery & equipment
- Non-Thai majority ownership allowed under BOI
- **Application:** 1-3 months approval, apply online at boi.go.th

## 2. Regulation & Licensing for EV Charging

### ERC (Energy Regulatory Commission) Requirements
- **ERC Notification 2022:** Public EV charging stations must register with ERC
- **Not a full energy license** — simplified registration process
- Required: Safety inspection certificate, meter calibration, insurance
- Annual reporting of kWh dispensed

### PEA Registration Process
1. Apply for dedicated meter (TOU tariff for EV charging)
2. Submit electrical safety inspection (by licensed engineer)
3. Register charger with ERC via PEA
4. Get EVSE ID assigned
5. Timeline: 2-4 weeks

### Standards
- **TIS 61851:** EV charging system safety (based on IEC 61851)
- **TIS 2749:** EV connector types — CCS2 (DC) + Type 2 (AC) = mandatory
- **TIS 62196:** Plugs, socket-outlets for EV charging
- Chargers must have CE/TIS certification
- Ground fault protection, emergency stop button required

### Foreign Ownership
- **Under BOI:** Foreigners can own 100% of EV charging company
- **Without BOI:** Foreign Business Act limits — need Thai majority (51%) or FBL
- **Recommendation:** Apply for BOI promotion — solves ownership + tax benefits
- Company type: Thai Co., Ltd. registered with DBD

### OCPP Protocol
- **Open Charge Point Protocol** — industry standard for charger management
- Enables: remote monitoring, pricing control, usage analytics, payment processing
- Version: OCPP 1.6 (stable) or OCPP 2.0.1 (newer)
- **Backend platforms:** Evolt, ChargeLab, Open E-Mobility, EVBox (SaaS ฿2,000-5,000/charger/month)

### Payment Systems
- QR code (PromptPay) — most common in Thailand
- Mobile app (custom or white-label)
- RFID card — for fleet/members
- Credit card terminal (optional, adds ฿5,000-15,000/unit)
- **Recommended:** QR PromptPay + App (lowest cost, highest adoption)

## 5. Business Models

### Model A: Full Ownership (RECOMMENDED)
- **You own everything:** chargers, backend, brand
- **Revenue:** 100% yours
- **CAPEX:** Higher upfront
- **Margin:** ฿5-10/kWh (with solar: ฿8-13/kWh)
- **Brand:** "TM Energy Charging" — builds your brand

### Model B: Network Partnership
- Partner with Evolt, EA Anywhere, or Sharge
- They provide: chargers, backend, maintenance, brand
- You provide: location, electricity, basic infrastructure
- **Revenue split:** 15-30% to location owner
- **Pros:** Zero CAPEX, professional operation
- **Cons:** Low margin, their brand not yours

### Model C: Hybrid Solar+EV (BEST FOR TM ENERGY)
- Install solar on location roof → feeds chargers
- Electricity cost drops from ฿4.5 to ฿0-2/kWh
- Sell both: solar installation + charging revenue
- **Double revenue stream:** EPC margin + ongoing charging income
- **Marketing:** "100% Solar-Powered EV Charging" — premium positioning

## 7. Importing EVs from China — Company Fleet

### Why Import Directly?
- Thailand dealer markup: 15-30% over factory price
- Direct import can save ฿100,000-300,000 per vehicle
- Fleet discount from factory: additional 5-10% off

### Top Models for Island Use
| Model | Battery | Range | Factory Price (CNY) | Thai Dealer Price (THB) | Est. Landed Cost (THB) |
|-------|---------|-------|--------------------|-----------------------|----------------------|
| **BYD Dolphin** | 44.9 kWh | 420 km | ¥96,800 (~฿480K) | ฿759,000 | ฿550,000-600,000 |
| **ORA Good Cat** | 47.8 kWh | 401 km | ¥109,800 (~฿545K) | ฿829,000 | ฿620,000-680,000 |
| **MG4 Electric** | 51 kWh | 350 km | ¥115,800 (~฿575K) | ฿869,000 | ฿650,000-720,000 |
| **BYD Seagull** | 38.8 kWh | 405 km | ¥69,800 (~฿347K) | Not yet in TH | ฿400,000-450,000 |
| **Wuling Mini EV** | 26.5 kWh | 200 km | ¥45,800 (~฿228K) | Not in TH | ฿280,000-320,000 |
| **Changan Lumin** | 31.4 kWh | 210 km | ¥49,900 (~฿248K) | Not in TH | ฿300,000-350,000 |

### Import Process
1. **Find factory/dealer in China** — Canton Fair, Alibaba, direct factory contact
2. **Order & payment** — L/C or T/T, usually 30% deposit + 70% before shipping
3. **Shipping** — RoRo or container from Chinese port to Laem Chabang, Thailand
   - Transit: 7-14 days
   - Shipping cost: ฿30,000-50,000/vehicle (container) or ฿15,000-25,000 (RoRo)
4. **Customs clearance** — Laem Chabang port
   - Import duty: **0% under China-Thailand FTA** (ACFTA) for qualifying EVs
   - Excise tax: **2%** (BEV)
   - VAT: **7%** on (CIF + duty + excise)
   - Total taxes: ~9-10% of CIF value
5. **Thai registration**
   - Emission test (TIS standards)
   - Safety inspection
   - DLT (Department of Land Transport) registration
   - License plate: ฿1,000-3,000
   - Insurance: mandatory third-party + recommended comprehensive
6. **Timeline:** Order to road: 10-15 weeks

### Can You Import Directly?
- **YES** — any Thai registered company can import vehicles
- Need: Import license from Customs Department
- Need: Type approval from DLT (or use models already approved in Thailand)
- **Shortcut:** Models already sold in Thailand (BYD, MG, GWM) have type approval
- **Risk:** Models NOT sold in Thailand need full type approval (~฿500,000-1,000,000 and 6+ months)
- **Recommendation:** Import models with existing Thai type approval (BYD Dolphin, MG4)

### Used EV Import
- **BANNED** — Thailand prohibits import of used vehicles (with very limited exceptions)
- Must be brand new, with certificate of origin

### Fleet Registration
- Register as company vehicles (รถยนต์นิติบุคคล)
- Tax deduction: depreciation over 5 years
- Fuel savings: ฿3-4/km (EV) vs ฿0.8-1.2/km (petrol)
- Maintenance savings: ~60% less than ICE vehicles

### Recommended Fleet for TM Energy (5 vehicles)
1. **2× BYD Dolphin** — Service/installation vehicle, good range
2. **2× BYD Seagull** (or Wuling Mini EV) — Island runabout, client visits
3. **1× BYD Atto 3** — Premium, client meetings, longer trips to Koh Samui

**Total fleet cost (direct import):** ~฿2,500,000-3,000,000
**vs. Thai dealer:** ~฿3,800,000-4,500,000
**Savings:** ฿1,000,000-1,500,000
